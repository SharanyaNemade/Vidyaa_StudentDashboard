Mathematics is the foundation of many fields and industries, from science and engineering to economics and everyday life. Let's dive into its various branches and explore how it shapes our world.

Key Mathematical Formulas

Pythagoras Theorem:

a² + b² = c²

This formula helps calculate the lengths of sides in a right-angled triangle.

Quadratic Formula:

x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a

Used to find the roots of a quadratic equation.

Great Mathematicians in History

Isaac Newton (1642–1727)

Known for his groundbreaking work in calculus and the laws of motion, Newton made immense contributions to the world of physics and mathematics.

Ada Lovelace (1815–1852)

A pioneer of computer science, Ada Lovelace is often considered the first computer programmer, and her work laid the foundation for modern computing.

Daily Use of Mathematics & Interesting Facts

Did you know? The Fibonacci sequence (1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, ...) appears in many natural phenomena, including the arrangement of leaves on a stem and the pattern of seeds in a sunflower.

Everyday Math: Whether you're calculating your grocery bill, splitting a dinner check, or estimating travel time, math is a part of daily life in ways you may not even notice.
